Grace Hopper's Career

Grace Hopper was a pioneer for computer programming and invented the term "computer bug ". She helped develop the first compiler that was a precursor to the widely used COBOL language and became a rear admiral in the U.S. Navy.(COBOL is a compiled English-like computer programming language designed for business use.) Skills and Talents: Yale awarded her a master’s degree in mathematics Published Works: Places Lived: New York City, New York Poughkeepsie, New York New Haven, Connecticut Northampton, Massachusetts Collaborators: Awards: Defense Distinguished Service Medal Legion of Merit Meritorious Service Medal American Campaign Medal World War II Victory Medal National Defense Service Medal Armed Forces Reserve Medal Naval Reserve Medal She reached the rank of Rear Admiral in the US Navy with a ship named after her In 2016, Hopper was posthumously honored with the Presidential Medal of Freedom by Barack Obama. The University of Missouri has honored Hopper with a computer museum on their campus, dubbed “Grace’s Place.”
